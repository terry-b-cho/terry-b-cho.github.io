About Me

I am a second-year PhD student in Bioinformatics and Integrative Genomics at Harvard Medical School, with a background in Economics and Mathematics. Although my early interests lay in physics, I ended up studying economics during undergrad, which taught me how to think in systems and patterns. I then transitioned into biomedical informatics, where I could apply computational and statistical tools to study complex biological questions. My current research centers on the genetic and computational dissection of microbial-host interactions and their co-evolution, with particular emphasis on the mechanisms and evolutionary dynamics that underpin symbiosis, pathogenesis, and immune modulation. I am especially interested in how these interactions influence the onset and progression of autoimmune diseases. Outside academia, I look for problems to solve, tools to build, or gaps to fill, whether it is something in science, tech, or everyday life.
